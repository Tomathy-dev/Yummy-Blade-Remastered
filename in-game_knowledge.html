Statistics

Statistics can be divided into 2 categories:

  • Base Statistics
  • Bonus Statistics

Base Statistics

Base Statistics are those that every champion have (manaless Champions being an exception), even without outside sources like items.

  • Health (HP): The amount of damage a Unit or Structure can take before Dying or being destroyed.
  • Health Regeneration (HP Regen): The amount of HP a Unit or Structure regenerates over a 5-second period. Effectively happens every half-second. It is considered a form of Healing.
  • Mana (MP): It is the most common resource Champions use to cast Abilities.
  • Mana Regeneration (MP Regen): Similar to HP Regen, but affects Mana instead of Health.
  • Energy: The equivalent of Mana for a few champions. cannot be increased.
  • Energy Regeneration: The equivalent of MP Regen for Energy users.
  • Attack Damage (AD): The amount of damage a Unit or Structure deals with Basic Attacks and some Abilities that also scale with AD.
  • Attack Speed (AS): The frequency of a Champion's basic attacks.
  • Attack Delay (or Attack Speed Offset) : A hidden Variable used to calculate a Champion's effective Attack Speed.
  • Armor: A Statistic that reduces the damage taken from Physical Damage.
  • Magic Resistance (MR): A Statistic that reduces the damage taken from Magical Damage.
  • Range: A measurement used to determine how far a Basic Attack or Ability can reach.
  • Movement Speed (MS): The rate at which a Champion travels across the map.
  • Gold Generation: The amount of Gold earned automatically over a 10-second period.

    • Bonus Statistics

    Bonus Statistics are those that only come from Abilities, Passives, Items, and Runes.

  • Ability Power (AP): The Attack Damage equivalent of most Abilities.
  • Cooldown Reduction (CDR): Reduces Abilities' Cooldowns by a percentage, usually up to 40%.
  • Magic Penetration (Magic Pen): Reduces the effectiveness of a Target's Magic Resistance.
  • Armor Penetration (Armor Pen): Reduces the effectiveness of a Target's Armor.
  • Lethality: Grants Flat Armor Penetration through a formula also involving both the source and the target's levels.
  • Critical Strike Chance (Crit): The chance a Champion has to inflict a Critical Strike with their Basic Attacks or some Abilities. Measured in Percentage.
  • Critical Strike Damage (Crit Dmg): The amount of Damage a Critical Strike does. Default is 200% Damage.
  • Tenacity: Reduces the duration of Crowd Control effects by a percentage, with few exceptions.
  • Life Steal (LS): The amount of Health a Champion restores as result of dealing Physical Damage on Basic Attacks or some Abilities. Measured in percentage.
  • Spell Vamp: The Equivalent of Life Steal but affects Abilities or Item Actives that deal Magic Damage. Measured in percentage.
  • Heal and Shield Power: Increases the effectiveness of Heals and Shields by a percentage.

Damage

Damage can be separated into 4 types:

  • Physical Damage
  • Magical Damage
  • True Damage
  • Pure Damage

Physical and Magical Damage are the most common types of Damage. True Damage is a rare type of Damage. And lastly, Pure Damage is the rarest of them all, only present in the Nexus Obelisk of each Base (Also known as Fountain Laser).

  • Physical Damage: A type of Damage that is is dealt by Basic Attacks, some Items, some Abilities and more. It is mitigated by Armor.
  • Magical Damage: A type of Damage that is is dealt by some Items, some Abilities and more. It is mitigated by Magic Resistance.
  • True Damage: A type of Damage dealt by rare Abilities. True Damage ignores Armor and Magic Resistance.
  • Pure Damage: A type of Damage that ignores Armor and Magic Resistance like True Damage, but also ignores any invulnerability and revive effects. The only possible resistance to this type of Damage is Untargetability.

Gold

Gold is League of Legends' In-Game Currency. It is used to buy items in the shop that provide Champions with bonus stats and abilities, which in turn is one of the main ways for Champions to increase their power over the course of a game.

At the beginning of the game, Champions are given starting Gold (500g), and can receive more Gold through various means. Actively, Champions acquire Gold through last hitting minions and monsters, destroying enemy turrets, and killing enemy Champions (or assisting in kills). Champions also passively generate Gold, which can be increased by items. Passive Gold gain does not activate until minions spawn.

Items can be sold for Gold in the shop, and any purchase can be Undone to completely refund the Item's cost, provided the Champion has not since left the vicinity of the Shop, dealt or received Damage, or used the Item.

Minions

Minions are Units that belong to one team or the other, spawn periodically from the Nexus and advance from one of the 3 Lanes towards the enemy Nexus. They automatically attack any enemy Unit or Structure they find in their way.

Their porpuse is mainly for the In-Game Economy to tilt in the favor of those that can effectively Last Hit, that is, to deliver the Killing blow on them. The Champion that kills a Minion earns Gold.

There are 4 types of Minions:

  • Melee Minions: Weak melee attackers.
  • Caster Minions: Weak ranged attackers.
  • Siege Minions: Strong ranged attackers. Has more Health than Melee Minions.
  • Super Minions: Strong melee attacker. Has more Health than Siege Minions. Only spawns after an enemy Inhibitor has been destroyed.

Jungle

The Jungle refers to any area of the Map that is not a Lane or part of either team's base, including the River that divides it.

Junglers rely on killing neutral monsters in the Jungle to keep up with their Laning teammates in terms of Gold and Experience. In a standard 5-on-5 game of League of Legends, 4 Players on a team will be Laners, and 1 player will be a designated Jungler.

Monsters are neutral Units that have Patience, that indicates it's willingness to fight. Patience is lost when the Monster is too far from it's spawn point. If the Monsters' Patience runs out, they'll rapidly return to their spawn point, whilst recovering Health at a rapid pace. Monsters do not "forget" Champions that hide in Bushes.

In each Map side (separated by the River) there are 4 Normal Jungle Camps, 2 Buff Camps and 1 Boss Jungle Camp. The Jungle is symmetrical around the center of the Map.

Normal Jungle Camps

  • Raptors: The Raptors' Camp is made of 5 small Raptors and 1 Crimson Raptor.
  • Murk Wolves: The Murk Wolves' Camp is made of 2 small Murk Wolves and 1 Greater Murk Wolf.
  • Gromp: The Gromp Camp is composed of 1 Gromp alone.
  • Krugs: The Krugs' Camp is made of 1 Ancient Krug and 1 Krug. But these spawn 2 of the weaker version after dying. Ancient Kurgs spawn 2 Krugs, and Krugs spawn 2 Mini Krugs.

Buff Camps

  • Blue Sentinel: A Neutral Monster that has Negative Magic Resistance. Grants Crest of Insight when killed.
  • Red Brambleback: A Neutral Monster that has Negative Armor. Grants Crest of Cinders when killed.

Crest of insight is a Buff which grants increased Mana and Energy Regeneration, as well as +10% Cooldown Reduction. The Buff is transfered to the one that kills the holder of the Buff.

Crest of Cinders is a Buff which grants Health Regeneration and causes the holder's Basic Attacks to Slow the enemy while also dealing True Damage over time. The Buff is transfered to the one that kills the holder of the Buff.

Boss Camps

  • Dragon Pit: In this alcove spawn 5 dragon types. For the first 35 minutes, spawn the Elemental Dragons. Every time they are killed they take 6 minutes to respawn. After the 35 minute mark spawn Elder Dragons. The Dragons are the Cloud Dragon, Infernal Dragon, Ocean Dragon, Mountain Dragon and lastly, Elder Dragon. When Slain, except for the Elder Dragon, they give permanent Buffs to the team that kills it. Respectively 25/50/75% Out-Of-Combat Movement Speed, 8/16/24% increased Attack Damage and Ability Power, Restores 4/8/12% Missing Health and Missing Mana over 5 seconds (after 8 seconds of not taking damage from Champions and Turrets), 10/20/30% additional damage as true damage to Epic Monsters and Turrets.
    The Elder Dragon's Buff increases by 50% the already existant Buffs, and grants True Damage over time on all Spells and Basic Attacks over 3 seconds, scaling with Elemental and Elder Dragons killed.
  • Baron Pit: In this alcove spawn the Rift Herald, once, and the Baron Nashor. The Rift Herald spawns at 10 minutes, and if not slain, disappears before the Baron Nashor comes to claim his spot. It only spawns once, and drops a Relic that gives Empowered Recall and the Ability to spawn a friendly Rift Herald that is very strong against Turrets to the user.
    The Baron Nashor is the most powerful Neutral Monster in League of Legends. It severely debuffs any Champion near the Baron Pit while it is Agressive, but it grants an amazing Buff to the living team members of the team that kills it. Hand of Baron grants Empowered Recall, up to 40 Attack Damage and 40 Ability Power. Additionally, Nearby Minions are greatly enhanced. The Buff gives a great Sieging and Team-fighting potential, and as such it is usually highly contested between both teams.

Turrets

These are statue-like towers that fire powerful physical damage attacks. They target the first enemy to step in range, but prioritize minions over champions. But if any enemy Champion damages an ally, that same enemy is immediatly focused by the Turret. Turret's damage to a champion increases each subsequent attack. Their defenses increase when there is no enemy minion around them. Even more is that Turrets grant true sight in the area around them, which basically means anyone on the enemy team can be seen while near an ally Turret.


Each team possesses 11 total Turrets, 3 guarding each Lanes and 2 protecting the Nexus. The farthest away a Turret is to the allied base, the lower its attack damage. Turrets do not regenerate Health, with the exception of the Nexus Turrets, the 2 Turrets who protect their allied Nexus.
